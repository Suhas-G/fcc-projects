J R R Tolkien

John Ronald Reuel Tolkien, born on 3rd January, 1892, was an English writer, poet, philologist, and academic. He was the author of the classic high fantasy works The Hobbit and The Lord of the Rings.
While many other authors had published works of fantasy before him,the great success of The Hobbit and The Lord of the Rings led directly to a popular resurgence of the genre. This has caused him to be popularly identified as the "father" of modern fantasy literature or, more precisely, of high fantasy. Read more...

Books

Best known for his The Lord of the rings, he is also the author of many other famous literary works like The Hobbit, Beowulf: The Monsters and the Critics and posthumously The Silmarillion, Unfinished Tales and The History of Middle-earth, many more, published by his son Christopher Tolkien.

Achievements

Tolkien also won many prestigious awards for his works.

International Fantasy Award

1957

The last International Fantasy Award was awarded that year to The Lord of the Rings as Best Fiction Book.

Commander of British Order

1972

Tolkien was appointed by Queen Elizabeth II a Commander of the Order of the British Empire in the 1972 New Year Honours and received the insignia of the Order at Buckingham Palace on 28 March 1972.

Locus Award

1978

Tolkien's novel 'The Silmarillion' was awarded the Locus Award for best fantasy in the year 1978

6th in The 50 greatest British writers since 1945

2008

Tolkien was placed sixth on the list of ‘The 50 greatest British writers since 1945’ which was published by ‘The Times’ in 2008.
